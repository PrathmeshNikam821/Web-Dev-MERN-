About Spiderman

Spider-Man is a fictional superhero created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko. He first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962. Spider-Man's real name is Peter Parker, a high school student who gained spider-like abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

Spider-Man possesses superhuman strength, agility, and reflexes. He can cling to most surfaces, shoot spider webs using wrist-mounted devices called web-shooters, and has a "spider-sense" that alerts him to danger.

Spider-Man is one of the most popular and enduring superheroes, known for his sense of humor and relatable struggles as both a superhero and an ordinary person.

Creation and development

Marvel Comics is an American comic book publisher founded in 1939 as Timely Publications. It is known for creating iconic superhero characters such as Spider-Man, Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, and the X-Men. Throughout its history, Marvel has been influential in shaping the comic book industry, introducing complex characters with human flaws and diverse backgrounds.
